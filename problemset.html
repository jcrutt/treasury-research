§01The bar

AI-native. Vertically integrated. Licenses acquired.

Corgi is the comp — an AI-native, full-stack insurance carrier that acquired its own regulatory stack instead of renting one, now worth roughly $2.6B. That, distilled, is the standard for every category in this book.

AI-native at the core. The operating work — underwriting, ledger, claims, compliance — runs on software the company built, not a vendor model in a wrapper.
Vertically integrated. The company replaces the incumbent's function and captures the margin, rather than reselling the incumbent under its own logo.
Licenses acquired. The founder becomes the regulated operator — the carrier, the exchange, the transfer agent. The license is the moat.
